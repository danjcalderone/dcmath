GRAMMIANS - MATRIX SHAPE
Grammians

The Grammians of a matrix $$ AA^T \qquad \qquad A^TA$$ define its the "shape" separate from it's orientation. The Grammians are always symmetric, positive semi-definite since $$ y^TAA^Ty = |A^Ty|_2^2 \geq 0 $$ $$x^TA^TAx = |Ax|_2^2 \geq 0 $$ Given this, the matrix square roots are always well-defined. $$ (AA^T)^{\tfrac{1}{2}}, \qquad \qquad (A^TA)^{\tfrac{1}{2}} $$ and actually more fundamental than the Grammians themselves. The parallels with the two norm of a vector \(x \in \mathbb{R}^n\) or the magnitude of a complex number \(z \in \mathbb{C}\) are strong. $$ |x|_2 = \sqrt{x^Tx}, \qquad |z| = \sqrt{z^*z} $$

Polar Decomposition

Grammians relate closely to the polar decompositions which break down a matrix into it's shape and orientation and is a precursor to the singular value decomposition (SVD). $$ A = (AA^T)^{\tfrac{1}{2}}(AA^T)^{-\tfrac{1}{2}}A $$ and, alternatively $$ A = A(A^TA)^{-\tfrac{1}{2}}(A^TA)^{\tfrac{1}{2}} $$

Pairwise Inner Products

Explicitly, for example, the elements of \(A^TA\) are given by the pairwise inner products between columns of \(A\). $$ A^TA = \begin{bmatrix} |A_1|_2|A_1|_2 & \cdots & |A_1|_2|A_n|_2\cos\theta_{1n} \\ \vdots & & \vdots \\ |A_n|_2|A_1|_2\cos\theta_{n1}& \cdots & |A_n|_2|A_n|_2 \end{bmatrix} $$ where $$ A = \begin{bmatrix} | & & | \\ A_1 & \cdots & A_n \\ | & & | \end{bmatrix} $$ and \(\theta_{ij}\) are the angles between the columns. Note that this matrix would stay the same if all the columns were rotated by the same amount. Explicitly, \(A^TR^T RA = A^TA \) for any rotation \(R\). Similarly, \(AA^T\) is defined by the relative orientation of the rows.
