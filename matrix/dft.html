DISCRETE FOURIER TRANFORM MATRIX
Discrete Fourier Transform Matrix

The discrete Fourier transform matrix forms the eigenvectors for circulant matrices which can be used to represent periodic signals. The DFT matrix is composed of roots of unity and has many symmetries. Perhaps most significantly it is unitary, \(F^*F = I\). Elementwise, the DFT matrix is given by $$ \Big[\ F \ \Big]_{k\ell} = \tfrac{1}{\sqrt{n}}e^{\tfrac{2\pi(k)(\ell)}{n}i}, \qquad k,\ell = 0,1,\dots, n-1 $$ (For the expanded form, see below.) As representations of periodic signals, all circulant matrices can be diagonalized by the discrete Fourier transform (DFT) matrix. The unitary nature of the DFT matrix is the finite dimensional version of Parseval's theorem, ie. that a signal has the same magnitude in the time and frequency domains. The DFT matrix is the eigenvectors of the shift matrix $$ S = FDF^* $$

DFT Matrix Expanded

$$ F = \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 & \cdots & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)}{n}i} & \cdots & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)}{n}i} \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)(2)}{n}i} & \cdots & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)(n-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)(n-1)}{n}i} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)(2)}{n}i} & \cdots & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)(n-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)(n-1)}{n}i} \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)(2)}{n}i} & \cdots & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)(n-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)(n-1)}{n}i} \\ \end{bmatrix} $$ Note the symmetries of this matrix.

Alternative Forms

Although, the DFT matrix is complex, it forms of the eigenvectors of many real matrices and thus the columns (and rows) of the matrix must come in conjugate pairs. To see the conjugate pairs, we note that roots of unity can be defined rotating in either direction and thus we have the relationship $$ e^{\tfrac{2\pi(k)(\ell)}{n}i} = e^{\tfrac{2\pi(k)(\ell-n)}{n}i} e^{\tfrac{2\pi k n}{n}i} = e^{\tfrac{2\pi(k)(\ell-n)}{n}i} $$ This allows us to express the DFT matrix as $$ F = \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 & \cdots & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)}{n}i} & \cdots & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)(-1)}{n}i} \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)(2)}{n}i} & \cdots & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)(-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)(-1)}{n}i} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(-2)(2)}{n}i} & \cdots & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(-2)(-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(-2)(-1)}{n}i} \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(-1)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(-1)(2)}{n}i} & \cdots & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(-1)(-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(-1)(-1)}{n}i} \\ \end{bmatrix} $$ and to note that columns \(\ell\) and \(n-\ell\) are conjugate pairs (1 and \(n-1\) are pairs, 2 and \(n-2\) are pairs, etc). We can rearrange the columns of \(F\) to put conjugate pairs together. $$ F' = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & \cdots \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(1)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(1)(-1)}{n}i}& e^{\tfrac{2\pi(1)(2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(1)(-2)}{n}i} & \cdots \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)(-1)}{n}i}& e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)(2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)(-2)}{n}i} & \cdots \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)(-1)}{n}i}& e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)(2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)(-2)}{n}i} & \cdots \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)(-1)}{n}i}& e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)(2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)(-2)}{n}i} & \cdots \\ \end{bmatrix} $$ Elementwise: $$ \Big[ \ F' \Big]_{k\ell} = e^{\tfrac{2\pi(k\ell)}{n}i}, \qquad k,\ell =0,\pm 1,\pm 2, \cdots, \pm n/2 $$ Note that \(F'\) and \(F\) are related by $$ F' = FP $$ for the appropriate permutation matrix \(P\). Circulant matrices can be semi-diagaonlized by the real orthonormal matrix \(\bar{F} = F'\tilde{W}^*\) $$ \tilde{F} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & \cdots \\ 1 & \sqrt{2}\cos\left(\tfrac{2\pi(1)}{n}\right) & \sqrt{2}\sin\left(\tfrac{2\pi(1)}{n}\right) & \sqrt{2}\cos\left(\tfrac{2\pi(1)(2)}{n}\right) & \sqrt{2}\sin\left(\tfrac{2\pi(1)(-2)}{n}\right) & \cdots \\ 1 & \sqrt{2}\cos\left(\tfrac{2\pi(2)}{n}\right) & \sqrt{2}\sin\left(\tfrac{2\pi(2)(-1)}{n}\right) & \sqrt{2}\cos\left(\tfrac{2\pi(2)(2)}{n}\right) & \sqrt{2}\sin\left(\tfrac{2\pi(2)(-2)}{n}\right) & \cdots \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 1 & \sqrt{2}\cos\left(\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)}{n}\right) & \sqrt{2}\sin\left(\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)(-1)}{n}\right) & \sqrt{2}\cos\left(\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)(2)}{n}\right) & \sqrt{2}\sin\left(\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)(-2)}{n}\right) & \cdots \\ 1 & \sqrt{2}\cos\left(\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)}{n}\right) & \sqrt{2}\sin\left(\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)(-1)}{n}\right) & \sqrt{2}\cos\left(\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)(2)}{n}\right) & \sqrt{2}\sin\left(\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)(-2)}{n}\right) & \cdots \\ \end{bmatrix} $$ Elementwise: $$ \Big[ \ \tilde{F} \Big]_{k\ell} = \begin{cases} \frac{\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{n}} \cos\left(\tfrac{2\pi(k\ell)}{n}\right) & \ell \ even \\ \frac{\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{n}}\sin\left(\tfrac{2\pi(k\ell)}{n}\right) & \ell \ odd \end{cases} $$ where $$ \tilde{W} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ 0 & W & \cdots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & \cdots & W \end{bmatrix}, \qquad W = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -i & i \end{bmatrix} $$ Note that both \(W\) and \(\tilde{W}\) are unitary.
