CIRCULANT MATRIX

A circulant matrix \(C \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}\) for a vector \(c \in \mathbb{R}^n\) consists of that vector shifted through all possible circular permutations $$ C = \begin{bmatrix} c_0 & c_n & c_{n-1} & \cdots & c_2 & c_1 \\ c_1 & c_0 & c_n & \cdots & c_3 & c_2 \\ c_2 & c_1 & c_0 & \cdots & c_4 & c_3 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ c_{n-1} & c_{n-2} & c_{n-3} & \cdots & c_0 & c_n \\ c_{n} & c_{n-1} & c_{n-2} & \cdots & c_1 & c_0 \end{bmatrix} $$ Circulant matrices are composed of powers of the shift matrix, \(S\) $$ S = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} $$ Specifically, $$ C = c_0I + c_1S + c_2 S^2 + \cdots + c_{n-1} S^{n-1} $$

Circulant matrices are quite commonly used in discrete signal processing to represent periodic signals.
Diagonalization

As representations of periodic signals, all circulant matrices can be diagonalized by the discrete Fourier transform (DFT) matrix, \(F \in \mathbb{C}^{n \times n}\). The DFT matrix is composed of roots of unity and has many symmetries. Perhaps most significantly it is unitary, \(F^*F = I\). $$ F = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 & \cdots & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)}{n}i} & \cdots & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)}{n}i} \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)(2)}{n}i} & \cdots & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)(n-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(2)(n-1)}{n}i} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)(2)}{n}i} & \cdots & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)(n-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-2)(n-1)}{n}i} \\ 1 & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)(2)}{n}i} & \cdots & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)(n-2)}{n}i} & e^{\tfrac{2\pi(n-1)(n-1)}{n}i} \\ \end{bmatrix} $$ Elementwise: $$ \Big[\ F \ \Big]_{k\ell} = e^{\tfrac{2\pi(k)(\ell)}{n}i}, \qquad k,\ell = 0,1,\dots, n-1 $$ The DFT matrix is the eigenvectors of the shift matrix $$ S = FDF^* $$ Intuitively, the DFT vectors are eigenvectors of forward motion in time. Since a circulant matrix is a sum of powers of the shift matrix it also can be diagonalized by the DFT matrix $$ C =c_0I + c_1S + c_2 S^2 + \cdots + c_{n-1} S^{n-1} $$ $$ C =c_0FF^* + c_1FDF^* + c_2 FD^2F^* + \cdots + c_{n-1} FD^{n-1}F^* $$ $$ C =F\Big[c_0I + c_1D + c_2 D^2 + \cdots + c_{n-1}D^{n-1}\Big]F^* $$
