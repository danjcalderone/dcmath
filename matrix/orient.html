MATRIX ORIENTATION

Each matrix has a canonical orientation given by $$ R = (AA^T)^{-\tfrac{1}{2}}A = A(A^TA)^{-\tfrac{1}{2}} $$ Note the relation to the two different Grammians. One can check that the rows and columns of \(R\) are orthonormal. $$ RR^T = (AA^T)^{-\tfrac{1}{2}}AA^T(AA^T)^{-\tfrac{1}{2}} = I $$ The range of \(R\) and \(R^T\) are equivalent to the range of \(A\) and \(A^T\) respectively. This gives the interpretation that the columns of \(R\) give an orthonormal basis for the range of \(A\) Similarly, the rows of \(R^T\) give an orthonormal basis for the range of \(A^T\).

Polar Decomposition

These orientation matrices are related to the polar decomposition as well. $$ A = (AA^T)^{\tfrac{1}{2}}(AA^T)^{-\tfrac{1}{2}}A = (AA^T)^{\tfrac{1}{2}}R $$ and, alternatively $$ A = A(A^TA)^{-\tfrac{1}{2}}(A^TA)^{\tfrac{1}{2}}=R(A^TA)^{\tfrac{1}{2}} $$
