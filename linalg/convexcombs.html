Linear Combinations
CONVEX COMBINATIONS

One specific type of linear combination that often arises is a convex combination. A convex combination of vectors is formed when we require the coefficients of a linear combination to all be positive and sum to one, ie. $$ A_1 x_1 + A_2 x_2 + \cdots + A_n x_n $$ with $$ \sum_i x_i = 1, \qquad x_i \geq 0 $$ Note that we also write this in a compact notation as $$ \mathbf{1}^Tx = 1, \qquad x \geq 0 $$ or even more simply we say that \(x\) live on a simplex \(x \in \Delta_n \). Visually, a convex combination of vectors live in the space between the vectors. For any two vectors \(A_1\) and \(A_2\), \(A_1 \tfrac{1}{2} + A_2 \tfrac{1}{2}\) is the vector halfway between the two vectors; for \(n\) vectors \(\sum_i A_i \tfrac{1}{n}\) is the center of the set of vectors ("average" vector of the set); etc. Different convex combinations are illustrated in the figures below.

SPAN

A convex combination between just two vectors is often written in the form $$ y = A_1 (1-\alpha) + A_2\alpha $$ with \(0 \leq \alpha \leq 1 \). We notice that if we start out with \(\alpha = 0\), \(y=A_1\). As alpha goes to 1, \(y\) moves from \(A_1\) to \(A_2 \). We note that, we can rearrange the above expression to explicitly show the direction \(y\) is changing as \(\alpha\) increases $$ y = A_1 + (A_2-A_1)\alpha $$ \(y\) is \(A_1\) plus some scaled version of the vector from \(A_1\) to \(A_2\)
