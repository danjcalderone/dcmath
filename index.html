ROTATION MATRIX

The inverse of a rotation matrix is its transpose $$ R^{-1} = R^T $$ This relationship is equivalent to the columns being orthonormal. Explicitly $$ R^{-1}R = R^TR =I $$ $$ R^TR = \begin{bmatrix} - & R_1^T & - \\ & \vdots & \\ - & R_n^T & - \\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} | & & | \\ R_1 & \cdots & R_n \\ | & & | \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ \begin{bmatrix} R_1^TR_1 & \cdots & R_1^TR_n \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ R_n^TR_1 & \cdots & R_n^TR_n \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & \cdots & 0 \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & \cdots & 1 \end{bmatrix} $$ Elementwise: $$ R_i^TR_j = \begin{cases} 1 & i = j \\ 0 & i \neq j \end{cases} $$ Visually this means the columns (and rows) of \(R\) are orthogonal vectors on the unit sphere.

Example: a counter-clockwise 2D rotation given by $$ R_{2 \times 2} = \begin{bmatrix} \cos \theta & - \sin \theta \\ \sin \theta & \cos \theta \end{bmatrix} $$
